Types of Bike Insurance Covers in India

When purchasing a two wheeler insurance policy, you will have to choose between a third-party liability-only insurance policy or a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy. Listed below are the main features and benefits of each type of insurance cover.

Third-Party Liability-Only Insurance Policy

A third-party liability-only insurance policy is the most basic type of insurance cover that you can purchase as a vehicle owner. It is also mandatory for vehicle owners to have, at the very least, a liability-only insurance policy at all times. These policies provide coverage if you injure a third party and/or their property/vehicle/assets in an accident. Accidents usually have legal consequences, and your liability-only insurance policy can protect you from expenses that you may have to incur as a consequence. Third-party liability insurance policies also offer a personal accident cover to the policyholder. However, these policies do not cover damage caused to the rider's own vehicle. As per the Motor Vehicles Act in India, it is mandatory for every vehicle to have at least a third-party liability cover.

The Coverage provided by a Third-Party Liability-Only Insurance Policy is as follows:

  • Damage caused to a third party's vehicle, assets, or property
  • Bodily injury or death caused to a third party
  • Personal accident cover for the owner/rider of the vehicle, up to a certain pre-specified sum insured

Comprehensive Two Wheeler Insurance Policy

As the name suggests, a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy offers the insured member a more enhanced coverage. All comprehensive two wheeler insurance policies come with third-party insurance coverage. In addition to this, comprehensive insurance policies also offer an own damage cover and personal accident cover. Thus, your vehicle is protected not only from accidents but also from natural calamities, man-made events, and theft of the vehicle.

A comprehensive bike insurance policy provides the following coverage to policyholders:

  • Third-party legal liability that covers bodily injury/death of a third party and/or damages to a third party’s assets
  • Personal accident cover for the owner/rider of the two-wheeler
  • Damage caused to one’s own vehicle due to an accident
  • Vehicular damages caused as a result of man-made events.
  • Vehicular damages caused due to lightning, fire, earthquake, flood, storms, etc.
  • Constructive total loss

Comprehensive vs. Liability-Only Insurance Cover - Which is better?

While purchasing a two-wheeler, you will have to choose between a third-party liability-only insurance policy or a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy. Either one of these two policies might be the right choice for you based on your requirements and circumstances. In terms of the cost, a liability-only bike insurance policy is usually inexpensive when compared to a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy. While a comprehensive insurance policy might be slightly more expensive to purchase, it is important to note that the coverage offered by this policy is also higher.

With a third-party liability-only insurance policy, if you meet with an accident and your vehicle is damaged, your liability-only policy will not cover the related expenses. You will have to repair your vehicle and bear the costs on your own. However, if the damage is caused to a third party's vehicle, your liability-only insurance policy will cover the resulting expenses. On the other hand, a comprehensive insurance offers you more enhanced coverage.

When purchasing a bike insurance policy, most people opt for a liability-only insurance policy simply because it is the cheaper of the two, without considering the inclusions and exclusions of each type of policy. A comprehensive two wheeler insurance plan offers extensive coverage, and it is usually better to have a policy that offers a larger coverage than meet with an unfortunate situation and then deal with financial troubles.

While it is necessary to consider the factors mentioned above, you must also take into consideration your lifestyle and needs. If you use your two-wheeler very minimally, you can get away with purchasing only a third-party liability insurance policy. Similarly, if you are sure that you will be able to pay for all the expenses that you may have to incur if you happen to meet with an accident, you can again opt for a third-party liability-only policy and pay a lower premium. However, if you frequently use your two-wheeler, it is advisable to purchase a comprehensive bike insurance policy.

  • Type of Policy: Your premium will directly correspond to the type of policy you have. A third-party liability-only policy will have a lower premium than a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy. However, the lower premium comes with a price. Third-party liability policies are not as exhaustive as comprehensive insurance policies. Hence, opt for a policy that fits your budget and your coverage needs.
  • Add-On Covers and Riders: Insurance companies offer individuals the option to buy several add-on covers. If you purchase a long-term policy and pay your premium as a one-time instalment, you may be eligible to claim a long-term policy discount. The third-party premium rate is fixed by the IRDAI (Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority in India), and they are usually raised every year. Under a long-term policy, third-party premium rates will remain frozen for the duration of the policy tenure, thus guaranteeing a lower premium payment.
  • Purchasing Policies from the same Insurer: If you are purchasing an insurance policy from one insurer, it is a good idea to also purchase other insurance policies from the same insurer.   • Online Purchase: While purchasing an insurance policy online can be beneficial to customers since it lessens the total paperwork needed and is time-efficient, it may also give you additional discounts on your premiums.   • Your Choice of Two-Wheeler: If you have purchased a luxury sports bike or a brand new imported bike, your insurer is likely to charge you more as premium since the cost for repair will be significantly more. However, if you purchase a standard bike with not too many frills included, your insurer may offer you a more competitive premium rate.
  • Compare between Insurers: Different insurance companies will provide you a different quote based on their coverage, their inclusions, exclusions, etc. It is vital that you compare between insurers and find a policy that has affordable premium payments.
  • Avoid Filing Claims for Small Damages: If you file a claim every small damage that occurs to your vehicle, your no-claim bonus could become nil. You will only be awarded a no-claim bonus if you don't make any claims during the policy tenure. Your no-claim bonus is instrumental in helping you secure a lower premium for the following year. Hence, If the damages are small and barely noticeable, get it fixed yourself and don't file a claim for the same.
  • Discounts based on Age and Experience: Insurers often look at the number of years you have been riding a two wheeler before fixing your premium. A young, inexperienced rider comes with more risk than a middle-aged, experienced rider. For this reason, ensure you show your insurer your number of years of riding experience and your current age. If these factors are in your favour, they will help you secure a lower premium.

If you have met with an accident or if your two wheeler has been damaged in an accident, you will have to file a claim with your insurer.   • Firstly, you will need to register your claim with your insurer. If the two wheeler or certain parts of the two wheeler have been stolen or damaged by miscreants, you will have to register an FIR with the police.

In case you need roadside assistance, you can contact your insurer and have your vehicle towed to the nearest garage.
  • Lastly, you will have to raise a claim with the insurer, submit the required supporting documents, and await your claim settlement. The status of the claim will be communicated to you by the insurer. However, you can also track the claim status directly on the insurer's website.

Constructive Total Loss

When you purchase a two wheeler insurance plans, the insurer will fix an IDV or Insured Declared Value. The IDV is considered as the sum insured for your two wheeler insurance, and this is the maximum amount that the insurer will be liable to pay you. Anything over the IDV amount will have to be settled by the insured individual. Sometimes, in case of an accident, especially if it's a head-on collision, the 2 wheeler might be completely wrecked. The cost of repairing such a vehicle might be more than the cost of purchasing the same model in the current market. During such times, your insurer may notify you that the cost of repairing your bike is more than the current cost of the two wheeler or more than the IDV. If the cost of repairing your bike is over 75% of the Insured Declared Value, it is considered to be a Constructive Total Loss. If this is the case, your insurer will pay you the IDV amount after taking depreciation into account.

Claim Settlement Ratio

The claim settlement ratio (CSR) refers to the total number of bike insurance claims that have been settled by the insurer in a particular financial year against the total number of claims they received during the same year. The higher an insurance company's claim settlement ratio is, the better are the company's chances of attracting policy buyers. The insurer's claim settlement ratio should be one of the deciding factors before purchasing a policy. A high claim settlement ratio is a good indication that you won't have to face any undue hassles to receive the claim. A higher claim settlement ratio is also a good indication of customer satisfaction.

A low claim settlement ratio might indicate the company unwillingness to settle claims for a range of reasons, including contractual binding, misrepresentation or mentioning incorrect information by the insured party, or fraud. The claim settlement ratio is reported by every insurance company to the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) every fiscal year, which is then published on the IRDAI website. In most cases, you will also find this information on the insurer's website. The CSR is calculated by dividing the claims settled by a company in a year with the number of claims it received, and multiplying this amount by 100.

What is a PUC Certificate?

If you own a two-wheeler, you are required to have a valid two-wheeler insurance policy, driving licence, and a Pollution under Control (PUC) certificate. Similar to how the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 makes it mandatory for vehicle owners to have a bike insurance policy at all times, getting a PUC certificate for your vehicle is mandatory as per the Central Motor Vehicles Rule, 1989.

The Pollution under Control certificate, also known as the PUC certificate, is a certification that is granted to vehicles after the vehicle passes the PUC test. Thus, getting a PUC certificate for your two-wheeler implies that your motorbike or scooter’s emissions are within the specified limits.

Why is it Necessary to Get a Pollution under Control (PUC) Certificate in India?

All motor vehicles that ply the roads, including two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and four-wheelers, can cause air pollution if the vehicular emissions are left unchecked. Thus, in order to ensure that the smoke that is released by motor vehicles is not a threat to the quality of the air in one’s city of residence, it is necessary to check the emission levels of the vehicle.

If the emission level is deemed to be safe, a Pollution under Control certificate will be provided to the vehicle owner. There are a number of pollution testing centres that issue PUC certificates for vehicles located all across the country, thus ensuring that vehicle buyers and owners can get a PUC certification for their vehicles with ease. The PUC test is conducted by pollution testing centres at nominal rates that usually range between Rs.60 and Rs.100, based on the type of vehicle that one owns.

Validity of the PUC Certificate

Once issued, the PUC certificate is valid for a period of one year. After the completion of this initial one-year period, you will need to get the PUC test done every 6 months. If the emission reading of your two-wheeler is higher than the approved limit, the PUC certificate’s validity will be decided by the concerned authority based on the test’s reading. If your two-wheeler’s emission levels are extremely high, the PUC testing centre may inform the concerned RTO in your district/city of the same.

Details Mentioned in a PUC Certificate

Certain basic details that are mentioned in the PUC Certificate are as follows:

  • Licence plate/registration number of your two-wheeler
  • Serial number of the PUC certificate
  • Date of when the PUC test was conducted
  • Pollution under Control test reading
  • The PUC certificate’s expiry date

Penalties in the Absence of a PUC Certificate

In India, riding a two-wheeler, or any vehicle for that matter, without a valid Pollution under Control certificate is a punishable act, as per Section 190(2) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Further, the individual riding a two-wheeler without a valid PUC certificate will have to pay a sum of Rs.1,000 for the first offence and a sum of Rs.2,000 for every subsequent offence.

Just like your driving licence and the two-wheeler insurance policy, you should also carry your two-wheeler’s PUC certificate with you when riding your two-wheeler. If the concerned authorities stop you and ask you for your vehicle’s PUC certificate, you should be able to produce it immediately if you wish to avoid being fined.

Return to Invoice Cover in Two Wheeler Insurance – What Does It Mean?

For example, if your two-wheeler gets stolen and the insurance company approves your claim, you will not receive the full purchase price of the vehicle as the claim settlement. What you will receive, instead, is a certain sum of money that has been adjusted for depreciation. Thus, with a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy alone, you will receive no more than the vehicle’s Insured Declared Value or IDV, which is based on the current market value of your vehicle.

In order to remedy this, motor insurance providers offer policy buyers the option to purchase a Return to Invoice Cover along with their policy. Thus, if you have the Return to Invoice Cover and you meet with an accident that is classified as a total loss by the insurer, the insurer will pay you the full invoice price of your two-wheeler.

One thing to keep in mind is that the Return to Invoice Cover is not offered to all two wheeler insurance policy buyers. Insurance companies offer this add-on to policy buyers who have new or relatively new bikes. Thus, if your vehicle is many years old, you might not be offered the Return to Invoice Cover since the effects of depreciation can no longer be ignored by the insurance company, at this point.

The Return to Invoice Cover will provide you with coverage against the following events:

  • Accidents that are classified as a total loss or a constructive total loss (CTL)
  • Theft of the vehicle
  • Loss of the vehicle

*Note: Inclusions might vary based on the insurer’s terms and conditions.

The following instances/events are usually not covered by the Return to Invoice Cover:

  • If the total loss/theft claim is not admissible under the policy’s coverage, the policy buyer will not receive a claim settlement based on the Return to Invoice cover alone.
  • In case of theft claims, certain insurance providers will not accept the claim unless a final investigation report is submitted to them.
  • The Return to Invoice Cover can also not be used to make two wheeler insurance claims for small accidents and minimal damages. This add-on is only useful in the event of a complete loss or vehicle theft.
  • In most cases, vehicles that are over 3 years will not be covered by the Return to Invoice Cover.

Compulsory and Voluntary Deductible in two wheeler insurance – What you need to know?

In motor insurance, while the majority of the claim amount is settled by the insurance company, a portion of this amount might have to be borne by the policyholder due to the deductible clause in the two wheeler insurance policy. The deductible, also known as excess, is a certain predetermined amount that will have to be paid by the policyholder when the concerned individual is raising a claim. Deductibles can be of two types – voluntary and compulsory. Read on to know more about the difference between the two types of deductibles.

Compulsory Deductible Voluntary Deductible
All two wheeler insurance policies have a compulsory deductible clause. Thus, for any claim that you raise, you will have to pay a part of the claim amount out of your pocket. The compulsory deductible is a fixed amount that is decided by the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority (IRDAI) based on the type of vehicle you have. The insurance company, thus, only pays out the claim amount after deducting the compulsory deductible amount from it. Since the compulsory deductible is mandatory for all policyholders to pay, insurance companies do not offer any discount to policyholders for paying this amount. Unlike the compulsory deductible, which is mandatory for all policyholders to pay, the voluntary deductible can be opted by policyholders over and above the compulsory deductible amount. Many policyholders who wish to secure a lower premium from the insurer opt for a voluntary excess. Opting for a high voluntary excess will result in a low premium payable, and vice versa. The amount that you can opt for as the voluntary deductible is usually dependent on the insured declared value (IDV) of your two-wheeler. Further, two wheeler insurance companies in India also give a discount on the own damage premium to policyholders who opt for a voluntary deductible.

When should you opt for a Voluntary Deductible?

Policyholders who are looking to save money on premiums or are extremely confident of their riding skills can opt for a voluntary deductible when purchasing a two wheeler insurance policy. Individuals who very rarely use their two-wheelers can also opt for a voluntary deductible since this will help them secure a lower premium payment. However, you should only opt for a voluntary deductible if you are sure that you will be able to bear your share of the expenses in the event of a claim.

Given that you might need to raise a claim with your bike insurance company at the most unexpected of times, you will need to ensure that you always have the sufficient funds in hand since the insurance company will only be paying the claim amount minus the compulsory and voluntary deductible amount. Thus, when purchasing a bike insurance policy or when renewing your two wheeler insurance policy, make sure to assess your budget, premium payment capacity, past claim history,riding skills, and then choose an adequate voluntary deductible, accordingly, if you wish to.

Top 7 Two Wheeler Insurance Myths Busted

A number of vehicle owners, even today, purchase two wheeler insurance policies only because the law makes it mandatory for them to do so. Myth 1: I don’t need a comprehensive two wheeler insurance policy since only the third-party liability insurance cover is mandatory.

Although the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 makes it mandatory for policy buyers to purchase at least a third-party liability insurance policy, it is advisable to purchase a comprehensive two wheeler insurance cover if you require a higher level of coverage.

Myth 2: I can’t transfer my two wheeler insurance provider without losing the No Claim Bonus

two wheeler insurance companies in India offer a No Claim Bonus to policyholders who don’t raise any claims during the policy term, in an effort to reward them for their safe riding. Myth 3: I can’t purchase two wheeler insurance online.

On the contrary, two wheeler insurance companies in India give policy buyers the option to purchase two wheeler insurance policies through online and offline channels. Myth 4: Pillion riders are covered under my two wheeler insurance policy.

Most individuals with two wheeler insurance assume that their policy also covers the pillion rider. Myth 5: two wheeler insurance is expensive.

two wheeler insurance is usually not very expensive, especially when you consider the level of coverage that the policy offers. Safety Tips to Observe while Riding

  • Avoid riding under the influence of alcohol: It is absolutely important that you keep yourself from riding your two wheeler when under the influence of alcohol or other narcotic substances. Not only will it cause harm to you, but it may also affect other people on the road for no fault of theirs. Ensure you have an alternate means of transport arranged if you plan to drink.

Ensure you always wear a helmet, even if you are riding only for a short distance. A helmet could be the only thing that keeps an accident from becoming fatal. A helmet with a good face and eye shield is of utmost importance when it comes to safeguarding yourself if you meet with an accident. It is also necessary for pillion riders to safeguard themselves with a good helmet. A protective helmet can prevent critical brain damage and even death.
  • Avoid distractions: Many people falsely believe that speaking on the phone or even texting while riding won't impair their judgement or affect their attention. Nothing can be farther away from the truth. Speaking on the phone or not paying sufficient attention to what's ahead can cause even the most experienced riders to meet with accidents. If you need to answer an important call or text, ensure you stop at the road side and do so, and start riding only after you have completed the said task. The same applies to listening to jarring music on your headphone. Distractions may be many, but ensure you keep your eyes on the road to protect yourself and others.
  • Avoid riding in bad weather: Riding in bad weather conditions can be quite risky. Heavy rain, fog, mists, and storms can greatly reduce road visibility and cause potential accidents. Slippery roads on rainy days are a major cause for concern since one has to be especially carefully while steering and manoeuvring. Strong gusts of wind can also make it hard to ride. If you do have to ride during bad weather, ensure you ride at a slower pace than usual.
  • Look out for road hazards: While potholes and excess sand and debris on the road is rather unpleasant while driving a car, it becomes so much worse if you are riding a two wheeler. Ensure you pay attention to the road ahead, so you can avoid these road hazards in a timely manner. If avoiding them is not an option, make sure you at least slow down so you get more control of your two wheeler.
  • Follow traffic regulations: Road safety should not be taken for granted. Traffic rules exist for a reason, and they should be followed by everyone. Don't skip that red light, and don't drive rashly just to save a few minutes. Not only will it make riding more dangerous for you, the rider, but it might also put others at risk. Following specified speed limits, stopping at traffic signals, etc. are a few simple things you can do when you are riding.
  • Wear the appropriate riding gear: Ensure to wear the right riding gear before heading out for a long ride. It is essential that you wear protective boots, a good quality leather jacket, gloves, anti-skid shoes, etc. Thus even if you were to meet with an accident or skid on the road, your clothing will help you stay protected and reduce the impact of the accident.

Riding classes will ensure that you know the correct riding strategies and are well-versed with the local traffic laws.   • Always stay on guard: It is wise to always ride defensively, no matter how experienced you are. Watch out for pedestrians, blind spots of other riders and drivers, and ensure you maintain a safe distance from other vehicles. Even when you follow all the necessary traffic regulations, accidents can happen due to somebody else's negligence. Hence, you must always keep your guard up when riding a bike.

Two Wheeler Insurance- FAQs

1.What is a deductible? Is it a good idea to opt for a Voluntary Deductible?

A deductible is a part of the claim that will have to be settled by the insured individual. The insurer will not pay for your share of the deductible. Your premiums are also linked to the deductible. Higher the deductible you opt for, the lower your premium will be. However, opting for a high voluntary deductible is smart only if you are sure that you will be able to pay for your share of the claim.

If you have lost your two-wheeler insurance policy, the first thing you will have to do is file an FIR at a police station. Next, you can apply for a duplicate insurance policy from your insurer. To do so, you will have to submit a written request along with any supporting documents that may be required by your insurer. Your insurer may also ask you to submit a signed indemnity bond.

3.If my two-wheeler is stolen or lost, how much is the insurer liable to pay me?

If your two-wheeler is lost or stolen, you are eligible to claim the IDV or Insured Declared Value of the two-wheeler. The IDV will be based on various factors, such as the cubic capacity of the engine, the age of the vehicle, and the geographic location. Also, your premiums are linked to the Insured Declared Value. If your two-wheeler has a high IDV, your premium will be more, and vice versa.

4.I have recently modified my bike. Am I still eligible to purchase a two wheeler insurance policy?

Yes, you can purchase two-wheeler insurance policies for modified bikes. Your premium amount may increase or decrease based on the type of modifications that are done. If the performance or aesthetics of the bike was modified, your premium may also increase. However, if you modified your two-wheeler to increase the security of the bike, then your two-wheeler insurance premium will decrease.

5. What is the importance of an endorsement for two wheeler insurance policies?

While purchasing a two-wheeler insurance policy, you are required to provide certain details about yourself and your vehicle to the insurer. If you have provided any wrong information while purchasing the policy, your claim could be denied by the insurer. Hence, if you realise that you have furnished any wrong details, you will immediately have to make an endorsement. Any changes requested by you once the policy has been issued will be incorporated by way of an endorsement by your insurer.

6. Can I change my insurer during the policy term?

Yes, you can port your insurance policy anytime. However, you will have to ensure that you notify your current insurer of your wish to port the policy and submit a written request 45 days before the expiry of the policy. 7. Do long-term two wheeler insurance policies come with any exclusions?

While the exclusion criteria will vary from insurer to insurer, a few general exclusions for long-term two-wheeler insurance policies are:

  • Wear-and-tear of the vehicle
  • Consequential loss
  • Depreciation
  • Breakdown caused due to mechanical or electrical failure
  • Damage/accident caused by someone riding without a licence
  • Damage due to war, nuclear risk, etc.
8. How is the Insured Declared Value determined?

The IDV or Insured Declared Value is the maximum sum assured that you can claim for. In case the vehicle is stolen or lost, the insurer will pay you the IDV. The IDV is calculated by reducing the depreciation value from the current selling price of the vehicle. If your vehicle is modified or fitted with accessories that were not installed by the manufacturer, the IDV for these accessories will be calculated separately. A few factors that will determine the Insured Declared Value of your two-wheeler are the vehicle type, vehicle make, the model, the state of registration, etc. When the insurer fixes your vehicle's IDV, ensure that this value is close to the current market value of your vehicle. You will also have to remember that if your two wheeler has a higher IDV, the premium payable is also likely to be high, and vice versa.

Yes, any security-related modifications that you make to your two wheeler will help you secure a lower premium. Security modification, like an anti-theft alarm, make it less likely for your two wheeler to be stolen. In turn, this will reduce the insurer's risk, which will help in reducing your premium. If you make the security modifications after you have purchased a two wheeler insurance policy, ensure that you immediately notify your insurer of this and have an endorsement made.

Apart from coverage following accidents, a comprehensive two-wheeler insurance policy also offers coverage for a damaged two-wheeler following a natural calamity such as an earthquake, flood, lighting, landslide, etc. It also offers coverage for man-made calamities such as a fire, riots, strikes, etc. Lastly, a comprehensive two-wheeler insurance policy offers compensation in case the vehicle is stolen or if it is deemed a total loss following an accident or calamity.

This policy provides coverage against death/bodily injuries suffered by a third party and damage caused to a third party’s assets.   • Bundled two wheeler insurance Policy: The bundled two wheeler insurance policy provides a 5-year third-party liability insurance cover and a 1-year own damage cover to new two-wheeler buyers.
