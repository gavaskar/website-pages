What is Credit Card? A credit card is a plastic card issued by a bank or a financial institution to borrow funds at the cardholder’s convenience. The maximum credit limit is typically pre-set by the bank at the time of card issuance. The cardholders are free to use the available funds as and when required. The maximum amount of funds can be used only for card purchases such as online transactions and offline transactions at merchant terminals. A small portion, however, is reserved for credit card cash withdrawal. The cardholder has the option to repay the borrowed amount within the grace period, during which no interest will be charged, or he/she can opt for revolving credit which allows carrying forward unpaid amount to subsequent months and also charges interest on the same until the due is settled in full. But are you aware that each element on a credit card has a purpose? This section will help you understand the significance of each and every element printed on the front and rear side of a credit card.Let's discuss the elements on the front-side of a credit card.
  • Credit card number: The 16-digit number is one of the prominent elements of a credit card. If you think it's a random, machine-generated number, you're wrong. Each digit or a set of digits of the number not only provide specific information but are also used to identify the authenticity of the card, just by doing a simple math.
    • The first digit of the 16-digit number is the Major Industry Identifier (MII) and indicates a specific industry that issued the card. For banking and financial institutions, the digits 4 and 5 are assigned by the regulatory. Hence most of the credit cards numbers issued in India begin with 4 and 5. While Visa credit cards begin with 4, MasterCard powered card begin with 5. American Express cards, on the other hand, begin with 3.
    • The next 5 digits along with the first digit represent the issuer or the bank. They are thus called Issuer Identification Number or Bank Identification Number (BIN). Each bank uses a different sequence of IIN. Hence by looking at the first 6 digits, one can identify the issuing authority.
    • The digits 7 to 15 contain the primary account number of the credit card. The number is unique for each cardholder. The number is crucial to identify the customer hence are often masked in all the bank communications.
    • The last digit, the 16th, is called the check digit as it is used to check the accuracy of other digits on the card. The last digit can be generated by applying a mathematical formula called the Luhn Algorithm to the previous digits. One wrong number will give a different result and that's how inaccuracy of the digits will be identified.
  • The logos and names on the credit card: The next prominent components on a credit card are the issuer's logo, the name of the card, card networks such as Visa, MasterCard, RuPay, etc., the cardholder's name, and the validity date.
  • The chip and the contactless icon: These are the new entrants, we can say. With the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) mandating the conversion of all magnetic stripe-only cards into EMV-chip-based cards, all the new credit cards issued after 31 January 2016 are chip based. If your card is upgraded, you can see an EMV chip on the front side.
    • Another latest feature that has been introduced by credit card issuers is the contactless payment feature. If your card has the feature, you will find a symbol similar to a Wi-Fi symbol on your card.Now let's move on to the back-side of a credit card.
  • Magnetic stripe: Despite the introduction of EMV chip-based cards, the magnetic stripe, which carries all the crucial account information, still exists till the time all the retail terminals in India are upgraded to accept chip-based cards. This means, even though you receive a credit card very recently, it's likely that you will see both chip and stripe on your card.
  • CVV: The CVV or Card Verification Value is a security code that is used as a fraud-prevention tool. The CVV is typically used for online or card-not-present transactions. While VISA and MasterCards have 3 digits in the CVV, Amex has 4 digits.
  • Signature box, hologram, and others: The signature box is another security feature on a credit card. Every cardholder needs to sign in the box to make the card legally valid. In case suspicious cases, the signature on the card will be cross verified with other signature proofs the cardholders carry.
    • The hologram is the glittering little sticker that prevents physical copying of the card. It contains various layers with images imprinted at different angles. Its design makes it hard to copy using a scanner or other instrument. Hence, it also acts as another security feature on the card.How to Use a Credit CardThough the primary functionality of a credit card is to use it only for card-only transactions, such as to purchase goods or services at retail merchant terminals or online, the cash withdrawal feature offered on most the cards, allows the cardholders to withdraw a portion of the amount as cash from ATM. However, in both of the cases, the cardholders need a PIN (personal identification number) to process the transaction. The credit card issuers generate a unique PIN for every credit card and share it with the cardholder once the card is issued. Earlier, banks used to send the PIN through PIN mailer. But now they prefer green PINs which are self-generated by the cardholder. The PIN generation process is different for each bank and the same will be communicated to the cardholder once the card is activated. Once you receive the PIN, you can start using your credit card. Especially at POS terminals, you need to input the PIN once your card is dipped/swiped and the amount is entered. While some online transactions may require the PIN, others may ask for other security info such as CVV or one-time password. For ATM withdrawals, you can follow the same process as you do to withdraw cash using your debit card. The same PIN needs to be entered when necessary.     Business credit cards are gaining popularity these days. These cards provide business owners with a revolving line of credit which is easily accessible to meet their short-term financial needs. A business credit card acts as a financial cushion for budding businesses who are not sufficiently funded. Though business credit cardholders get a monthly statement, most banks also allow the employer to track their expenses online like check their year-end account summary, categorize their expenses, run an audit, and pay their taxes. Holding a business credit card helps businesses build a positive credit score if they pay their dues on time, which, in turn, will help them qualify for a line of credit in the future if the need arises.

    What Happens after I Swipe My Credit Card at a Merchant’s POS Machine?

    Of course, the million dollar question. While the mechanics of using a credit card on a day-to-day basis isn’t too complicated, the associated back-end process associated with a simple card swipe is too technical for a common individual to comprehend. In actuality, the procedure is very simple and safe-

    1. Purchase over, you hand the credit card to the retailer to pay for your purchase. He swipes, with an accompanying smile, of course.
    2. The merchant’s Point of Sale (POS) system is connected to the merchant’s bank via a communication link.
    3. Thanks to the POS system, your details are verified in real-time using the electronic verification system by the merchant’s bank. The verification is with regards to your credentials, validity of the card and availability of sufficient balance to pay for the purchase.
    4. Post the verification from the merchant’s bank, the details are forwarded to your bank for payment authorization if all the information is in order.
    5. When the merchant’s bank receives this authorization, it blocks the requisite amount from your credit limit to account for the purchase later on. Next, it generates an approval code that is transmitted to the merchant.
    6. At the merchant outlet where you stand for the clearance to affect quickly, the retailer receives this code and must key it in to obtain two charge slip copies from the POS system. One of these slips signifies your agreement with the card issuing bank to pay for the current purchase. You sign on this slip and return it back to the retailer. The other copy is yours to take away from this transaction.
    7. Safe, comprehensive and fast, all of these steps were affected in less than 2 minutes. Neat, right?

    Borrowing from a popular axiom, ‘With great power comes great responsibility’, and the same holds very true in the case of credit cards. While a handy financial help, a source of ready cash and a safe and beneficial way to pay for your purchases, the misuse of this facility could be detrimental to your long term financial health. Credit card debt, defaulting on your monthly repayments or drawing up a massive pile of credit card purchases and consequently, a stiff interest rate are things to be constantly aware of. The best way to enjoy your credit card is responsibly and with caution and tolerance. While the temptation may be high to unleash a shopping spree with your brand new credit card, remember, the money you spend is not your own- it must be returned back, with interest and on time!!

    Credit Card Shopping Checklist

    Credit Cards are a convenient financial option if you don't want to carry too much cash with you on that shopping spree. Here are a few factors to consider when you choose your perfect Credit Card.

    Interest Rates

    Interest on your Credit Card is charged on a yearly basis. It is a fee that you pay for using the card. It's a good idea to opt for a card with a fixed interest rate that remains the same every month.

    Annual Fees

    Credit Cards have an annual fee that is charged to the cardholder. Some charges that are included as a part of the annual fee are late payment charges, cash advances, any requests for increased credit limit and balance transfer.

    Grace Period

    A grace period is the number of days you will get to pay your Credit Card bill after the due date. The grace period is usually 30-50 days to clear any outstanding dues.

    Cash Withdrawal

    Credit Cards come with an ATM PIN so that you can get cash through your Credit Card from an ATM. But, remember that cash withdrawals on your Credit Card attract high interest rates.
